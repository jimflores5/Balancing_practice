**Page_2
Identifying the reaction type helps us predict missing formulas.
Synthesis reactions produce a <strong>single product</strong>, so look for ONE compound on the right side of the reaction.
Decomposition reactions start with a <strong>single reactant</strong>, so look for ONE compound on the left side of the reaction.
Singe replacement reactions have <strong>an element and a compound</strong> on BOTH sides of the reaction. For example:<br> <strong>Mg + AgNO<sub>3</sub> -> ____ + ____</strong> shows 2 products, plus an element and a compound on the reactant side. The reaction is single replacement!  
In double replacement reactions, <strong>two ionic compounds</strong> trade cations to produce two new ionic compounds. For example:<br> <strong>Cu(NO<sub>3</sub>)<sub>2</sub> + Na<sub>2</sub>SO<sub>4</sub> -> ____ + ____</strong> shows 2 reactants and 2 products, so the reaction is neither synthesis nor decomposition.<br> Since neither reactant is an element, the reaction is not single replacement.
Combustion reactions <strong>MUST have O<sub>2</sub></strong> as one of the reactants, and each product must contain some number of O atoms.
