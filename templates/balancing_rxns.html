Rearranging Atoms

  • Matter can be neither created nor destroyed in a normal chemical process.
  • Atoms are only rearranged during a reaction.

Conservation Questions

  1. For the reaction 4Fe + 3O2 -> 2Fe2O3, 1.65 g of iron reacts to form 2.36 g of Fe2O3. What mass of O2 reacted?
  2. A student dissolves 1.0 g of table salt (NaCl) into 50.0 g of water (H2O). The mass of the mixture after dissolving is ___.
    1. More than 51.0 g
    2. Less than 51.0 g
    3. 51.0 g

  1. Mass of reactants = Mass of products, so g Fe + g O2 = g Fe2O3.
    2.36 g Fe2O3 - 1.65 g Fe = 0.71 g O2
  2. C!!!!
    The NaCl did not disappear, nor did the water gain anything other than the added salt.
